Frequently asked questions

A bitmap is a type of image made up of a grid of individual pixels, each containing color or grayscale information.

Common bitmap formats include BMP, PNG, JPEG, GIF, and TIFF.

Bitmap images can be large because they store color information for every pixel, leading to large file sizes, especially at higher resolutions.

Pixel density refers to the number of pixels in a given area, often affecting the image's sharpness and clarity.

Bitmaps lose quality when scaled up because enlarging the image makes the individual pixels more noticeable, resulting in pixelation.
